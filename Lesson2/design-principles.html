Jovani Ambriz

Visual Hierarchy

Disney

www.disney.com

This website is a good example of the "Visual Hierarchy" principle. Your eye is drawn to the the order of what to focus on in a sequential order.

  1. The image of the Might Ducks team on the zamboni
  2. The title of the show.
  3. "Stream Now" button so it is easy for the viewer to start watching the movie.
  4. Description of the series.
  5. The links to other pages on the website.

White Space

Best Buy

www.bestbuy.com

The Best Buy website is a good example of how to utilize "white space" to create a distinction between the elements to help the user focus on the products that they are highlighting. The amount of white space also helps make the page more asymmetrical also they established a visual hierarchy. You are first drawn to the image of the iMacs and the second thing that stands out is the "shop now" button and that is accomplished by increasing the padding inside the button.

P.A.R.C Repetition

Adidas

www.adidas.com

The Adidas website has a good example of P.A.R.C repetition principle. It starts off with having a banner running across the width of the screen then it transitions into individual links to other parts of the website for members only, members weekly specials, member rewards, member prizes and so on. Then it transitions to another banner spanning the width of the screen.
